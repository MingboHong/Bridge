\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}
In this work, we investigate VFMs-based DGOD, revealing that single-source, limited-data training may risk spurious correlations. This motivates us to design \textbf{\textit{Bridge}}, a basis-driven DGOD framework that blocks confounders through causal inference. 
\textbf{\textit{Bridge}} can be seamlessly integrated with both discriminative and generative VFMs in a plug-and-play mechanism with end-to-end training along with the frozen VFM backbones.
Through extensive experiments on five comprehensive benchmarks spanning ground-level scenes to remote sensing imagery, diverse weather conditions, and visual styles, \textbf{\textit{Bridge}} establishes new state-of-the-art performance, demonstrating superior robustness across different visual domains. 
Also, we believe that our extended weather annotations on an existing UAV dataset offer a novel benchmark for domain generalization, contributing to future research in the remote sensing community.
